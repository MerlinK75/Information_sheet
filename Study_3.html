1. Invitation Paragraph
You are invited to take part in a research project. Before you decide whether to take part, it is
important for you to understand why the research is being done and what participation will involve.
Please take time to read the following information carefully and discuss it with others if you wish.
Please ask us if there is anything that is not clear or if you would like more information. Take time to
decide whether or not you wish to take part.
2. What is the projects purpose?
The project aims to develop an understanding of users brain state changes during meditation and
how the audio phenomenon binaural beats can affect these brain state changes.
3. Why have I been chosen?
Inclusion criteria: We are asking a healthy adult who is at least 18 years of age, have little to no
experience with meditation (novice meditator), and able to consent to volunteer for this study.
Exclusion criteria: Individuals with obstructions on their head, such as wearing a wig, weave, braids,
or any hair type that may be considered too dense for the EEG gel to pass through. The user should
have corrected vision. Additionally, participants are requested to abstain from alcohol, caffeine,
tobacco, and recreational drugs 4 hours before each session as these substances are known to affect
EEG activity.
[Voluntary Participation and Confidentiality for a participant who is currently a student of any member
of the research team]:
Your participation in this study is entirely voluntary. Choosing not to participate will have no impact on
your academic standing, grades, or relationship with teaching staff. If you are a student currently
taught or supervised by any member of the research team, please be assured that:
- Recruitment is being conducted by a neutral third party (e.g., a teaching assistant or administrator

- The research team will not know who has participated until after all assessments have been
completed and final grades submitted.
- All data will be anonymized or pseudonymized before analysis.
You may withdraw from the study at any time without giving a reason.
4. Do I have to take part?
It is up to you to decide whether or not to take part. If you do decide to take part, you will be given this
information sheet to keep and be asked to sign a consent form. You will also be reminded that you
can withdraw from the study at any time during the duration of the experiment.
You can also withdraw data you have provided, up to 5 days after the last data you submitted, without
giving a reason. If you choose to withdraw, the data will no longer be used. You will not in any way be
penalised for withdrawing from the study. Please note that there is an exception: focus group data
cannot be withdrawn as it is not feasible to isolate and remove an individuals contributions from the
group-based collective discussion.
5. What will happen to me if I take part?
You will be asked to use sensors (EEG and eye tracking) that will capture and record your neural
activity in an indoor, quiet space while meditating for periods that add up to approximately 2 hours.
This will be broken up by 1 hour on two separate days which will be organised to suit your availability.
You can take a break or stop participating in the study any time you need.
2
For lab studies (including in-person interview): the researcher may need to help you put on some of
these sensors. These may require physical contact. You will be explained this in advance and ask if
you feel comfortable with it. If you do not feel comfortable, you can decide to withdraw from the study
without giving an explanation or be penalized. No sensors will need to be worn under clothes.
For online surveys: you will be asked to answer questions on your experiences of using technologies
(e.g. wearable sensors) to manage your wellbeing. GDPR compliant software, such as, Microsoft
Forms and Qualtrics will present questionnaires and collect your responses to questions.
Tasks
First, you will be asked to fill out surveys to allow the researcher to understand your meditation
experience and impulsivity score. We would additionally ask you for demographic information (gender,
age, ethnicity, dominant hand), as some factors may affect the feedbacks effectiveness. You do not
have to answer any questions you do not feel comfortable with answering. The researcher will need to
help you put on the EEG which may require physical contact. You will be explained this in advance
and ask if you feel comfortable. If you do not feel comfortable, you can decide to withdraw from the
study without giving an explanation or be penalized. The EEG is administered on the head (like a hat)
causing light pressure, and gel is applied which may appear cold for the user which both may cause
discomfort for different individuals.
After equipping the EEG as stated above, you will be asked to complete an eye tracking task, where
an eye tracker camera will track your eye movement and pupil size. The eye tracker will not record
photos or images of your face, but only record data related with your eye movement and blink rate.
The task will measure your sustained attention during a Mackworth clock task where you will follow
the clock hand and you will need to notice when the clock hand skips a second. Afterwards, you will
be asked to meditate in a quiet room with noise cancelling headphones for a period of 4 minutes. If
you are unsure how to meditate, the researcher will discuss proper techniques on focused attention
meditation where you will be focusing on your breath. The meditation task will be followed with some
time to rest and questions on your overall experience through surveys and open-ended questions
from the researcher. These questions may be recorded so they can be transcribed for analysis with
the original recordings and being deleted after transcribing. If you dont give consent to the audio
recordings, the researcher will record on paper key points of your answers instead. Once rested, you
will be asked to meditate again with audio feedback for 4 separate sessions lasting 4 minutes. Once
each session is completed, you can remove the headphones and EEG and you will be asked
questions similar to the previous rest period, but this time on the feedback and how it was in
comparison with no feedback. After this session has been complete, the researcher will book the next
session for you on a date and time thats convenient. The next session will replicate this experiment,
the only difference being a different audio feedback mechanism will be used which has the same goal
of assisting with meditation but achieves this through a different methods/policy.
The EEG and headphones will be cleaned before you use them and will be cleaned after use as well.

7. What are the possible benefits of taking part?
Whilst there are no immediate benefits for those people participating in the project, it is hoped that this
work will assist general populations to start a meditation habit who struggle to start this habit without
assistance.
8. What if something goes wrong?
Extreme care will be taken in this research. However, if you wish to complain or have any concerns
that are not addressed by the researcher, you should contact Prof. Youngjun Cho
(youngjun.cho@ucl.ac.uk) who is the Principal Investigator on the project. If you further feel that your
complaint has not been handled to your satisfaction, you can contact the Chair of the UCL Research
Ethics Committee (ethics@ucl.ac.uk).
9. Will my taking part in this project be kept confidential?

10. Limits to confidentiality
Confidentiality will be respected unless there are compelling and legitimate reasons for this to be
breached. If this happens, we would inform you of any decisions that might limit your confidentiality.
11. What will happen to the results of the research project?
The findings of our analysis of the data collected from the participants of the research project will be
published in reports and articles and presented at public engagement and research talk venue. You
will be able to access academic publications of these findings on the principal investigators research
website: https://profiles.ucl.ac.uk/54637-youngjun-cho. You will not be identifiable in these
publications and presentations, except if you give us permission to include examples from the videos
we collect from you. In any case, your name or contact details will never be included in publications
and presentations.
The sensor data we collect from you and your self-reports, neither of which can be used to personally
identify you, will be made open for use by other researchers for the benefit of further research; but
only if you give us permission to do so.
